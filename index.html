Community Action to Improve our Internet Service

Update 21st Sep
After submitting the addresses and telephone numbers of those who expressed support of a Hollingbourne Hill cabinet to the Openreach, they responded on 21st Sep with the below:
Thank you for providing the list of addresses you wish to be included in the Community Fibre Project. We have now done an initial investigation and gathered the info we need in order to send this over to our planning team to provide an initial cost (ball park figure) – this may take 10-15 working days.

Once these costs have been determined, we can move on to the next stage. Some areas may be eligible for vouchers to part fund a project. Please note, this is not something Openreach have any power over and it is down to the Department of Digital, Media, Culture and Sport (DCMS). The vouchers are based on area and eligibility.

We will keep you informed throughout the process and let you know of any further information we need as we progress.
Once I receive this from Openreach I will provide an update on here.

It has also been brought to my attention by one of the members of our community that we may be eligible for further, additional funding via KCC’s ’Top-Up Voucher’:
From September 2020 residents in Kent will be able to claim up to £5,500 per residence and up to £3,500 per businesses. The Kent Top Up scheme can only be used in conjunction with the Rural Gigabit Voucher Scheme and will give both residential and businesses a combined value of up to £7,000 available per eligible premises.
This substantial increase in available funding should strengthen our position further.
Grants worth £2,500 per residential property, and £3,500 per business, are available now to improve our internet service.

Fellow residents of Hollingbourne Hill and the surrounding postcodes,

Properties and businesses listed within the postcodes; ME17 1QJ, ME17 1QL and ME17 1QR all currently receive internet speeds of less than 10 Mbps.

This is due to the 2.5 - 3.2 km distance between these postcodes and the Hollingbourne exchange located at 78 Eyhorne Street.

Whilst the majority of the village can receive speeds of up to 74 Mbps, everyone located around the top of Hollingbourne Hill and beyond, are unable to achieve anywhere close to those speeds.

Due to the relatively small number of properties at the above postcodes, in addition to their distance from the exchange, previously it has not been commercially viable for Openreach/BT to install a cabinet within our area, to provide an improved internet service.

However, as part of the UK Government’s election pledge to bring broadband speeds of up to 1,000 Mbps to the whole country by 2025, rural premises with broadband speeds of less than 100 Mbps can use vouchers worth £1,500 per home and up to £3,500 per business, to support the cost of installing new fast and reliable service. This is via the Government’s Rural Gigabit Connectivity Programme.

Kent County Council will also top up these voucher payments to residential properties by an additional £1,000, bringing the total available funding per property to £2,500.

Considering we all receive just 10 Mbps or lower, this is our opportunity to improve our internet speed and reliability, as a community.

This is required to support in an increasing number of internet-capable devices in the home, which include:

Mobile Phones - Whatsapp, FaceTime
Desktop Computer & Laptops
iPads and Tablets
Smart TVs - Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, BBC iPlayer, ITV Player, Now TV, Youtube, BT TV

All of these devices compete for the same limited bandwidth, and basic broadband services with slow speeds is increasingly becoming a major bottleneck.

The recent COVID-19 lockdown measures have highlighted this issue further. Keeping in contact with families during these difficult times (often via the internet) was made harder due to sluggish speed and reliability. Equally, businesses in the local area that rely on the internet may have experienced a further obstacle to operate.

Additionally, a recent survey by Internet Service Provider TalkTalk found 62% of British people admit they would consider NOT buying a house if it didn’t have a “strong, reliable internet connection”. Overall 53% of respondents said they consider a “fast, reliable internet connection” a priority when house hunting, which has almost doubled to 93% when answered post COVID-19.

As mentioned previously, the cost of installing a local cabinet to serve us all has not been viable due to the cost of installation, which on a UK average basis is £27,500. (It should be noted this is an average across the UK and is dependant on the number of properties, location etc)

Given the grants (£2,500 per home, £3,500 per business) available by both the Government and KCC, this cost may be completely covered. It will however be dependant upon us, as a community, coming together, to demand a faster connection. Pooling all of our grants/vouchers together will increase the chances of a new cabinet being installed locally, providing us all a reliable and fast internet connection.

A final cost will not be given by Openreach/BT until all properties and businesses requesting a faster connection have been accounted for.
In order to receive this quote, each property and business that wishes to benefit from an improvement of speed and reliability of their internet service, needs to provide their address and landline number, which will be passed on to Openreach/BT. By providing your address and telephone number, Openreach/BT can accurately assess the cost, demand, and the total value of all grants. This is a requirement to receive a quote and is in no way an obligation of any form.

Once a quote has been received, and the total amount of Government and KCC funding is totalled, we can, as a community, assess our options going forward. The more properties/businesses that express an interest, the likelihood of the total cost being covered by grants, is increased.
